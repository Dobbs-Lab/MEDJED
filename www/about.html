MEDJED

MEDJED (Microhomology Evoked Deletion Judication Elucidation) is a tool for assessing the probability of microhomology-mediated end joining repair at a particular DNA double-strand break site.

MEDJED is a random forest method trained using 5-fold cross validation on a set of 66 HeLa cell targets. It uses sequence features including the predict the proportion of deletion events due to MMEJ repair.


On an independent test set of 23 HeLa cell tragets, MEDJED achieves a mean absolute errror (MAE) of 10.26%, root mean square error (RMSE) of 12.02%, and Pearson Correlation Coefficient (CC) of 85.20% when comparing the predicted proportion of MMEJ repair outcomes with the observed proportion of MMEJ repair outcomes.
